Our Technology

Pioneering brain-state modeling using electroencephalography (EEG) to bridge human thought and technology.

EEG Technology Overview

At Neuvelon, we're exploring the intersection of neuroscience, signal processing, and embedded systems to create revolutionary technologies that interpret and utilize brain activity. Our research focuses on brain-state modeling using electroencephalography (EEG), a non-invasive method to record electrical activity of the brain through electrodes placed on the scalp.

We've developed a novel software framework that can analyze EEG signals to model different brain states and utilize these decoded signals to control embedded systems and microcontrollers, opening up new possibilities for human-computer interaction and assistive technologies.

Our Technology Process

1

EEG Data Acquisition

We use high-resolution EEG equipment to collect data from subjects in various mental states. The equipment consists of multiple electrodes, amplifiers, and a data acquisition system.

Data collection involves placing the electrodes according to the international 10-20 system, ensuring proper contact and minimal impedance. Sessions are conducted in a controlled environment to reduce external noise and artifacts.

2

Signal Processing

Processing raw EEG data involves several key steps to ensure accuracy and reliability. Initially, preprocessing techniques such as band-pass filtering are applied to remove noise and artifacts.

This is followed by advanced methods like Independent Component Analysis (ICA) to separate and eliminate eye blink and muscle artifacts. Feature extraction is performed using techniques such as power spectral density analysis and wavelet transforms.

3

Brain-State Modeling

Brain-state modeling aims to identify and characterize various mental states based on EEG signals. This involves developing algorithms that can reliably distinguish between states such as relaxation, concentration, and stress.

Neural networks and Support Vector Machines (SVM) are among the algorithms used for this purpose. The process begins with preparing training data, which includes labeled EEG recordings corresponding to different brain states.

4

Signal Decoding

Signal decoding involves converting identified brain states into control signals that can be used to operate embedded systems and microcontrollers. This translation process relies on specific algorithms designed to map EEG-derived features to control commands.

Challenges in decoding include ensuring low latency and high accuracy, as real-time control applications demand immediate and precise responses. Strategies such as adaptive filtering and dynamic thresholding are employed to enhance decoding performance.

5

Software Development

The software platform developed in our research integrates EEG signal analysis and embedded system control into a cohesive tool. The architecture consists of several key modules, including data acquisition, signal processing, brain-state modeling, and signal decoding.

Key features of the software include real-time analysis, a user-friendly interface, and extensive customization options for different applications. Integration with EEG hardware is achieved through standardized protocols.

6

Embedded System Control

Controlling embedded systems with decoded EEG signals demonstrates the practical applications of our research. Various systems, such as robotic arms, home automation devices, and assistive technologies, are used to showcase this capability.

Implementation involves programming microcontrollers to respond to control signals generated by the software platform. For example, a robotic arm can be directed to move based on the user's mental commands, facilitating tasks for individuals with disabilities.
