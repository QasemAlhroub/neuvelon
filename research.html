Research

Exploring the frontiers of brain-state modeling and EEG technology to advance neuroscience and human-computer interaction.

Our Research Focus

At Neuvelon, our research is centered on brain-state modeling using electroencephalography (EEG), exploring the intersection of neuroscience, signal processing, and embedded systems. We're developing novel software frameworks that can analyze EEG signals to model different brain states and utilize these decoded signals to control embedded systems and microcontrollers.

Our interdisciplinary approach combines expertise in neuroscience, computer science, electrical engineering, and human-computer interaction to create innovative solutions that bridge the gap between human thought and technology.

Key Research Areas

EEG Signal Processing

Developing advanced algorithms for filtering, cleaning, and extracting meaningful features from raw EEG data. Our research includes:

  • Artifact removal techniques
  • Wavelet transforms for time-frequency analysis
  • Independent Component Analysis (ICA)
  • Adaptive filtering methods

Brain-State Classification

Creating algorithms that can reliably identify and classify different mental states from EEG signals. Our research includes:

  • Neural network architectures for EEG classification
  • Support Vector Machines (SVM) optimization
  • Deep learning approaches for brain-state recognition
  • Transfer learning for improved classification accuracy

Real-time Signal Decoding

Developing methods to translate brain states into control signals with minimal latency. Our research includes:

  • Low-latency processing pipelines
  • Adaptive thresholding techniques
  • Predictive algorithms for anticipatory control
  • Optimization for embedded system implementation

Brain-Computer Interfaces

Creating intuitive interfaces between the brain and external devices. Our research includes:

  • User-centered BCI design methodologies
  • Multimodal integration with other biosignals
  • Adaptive interfaces that learn from user behavior
  • Feedback mechanisms for improved control

Research Methodology

Data Collection

We use high-resolution EEG equipment to collect data from subjects in various mental states. Data collection involves placing electrodes according to the international 10-20 system, ensuring proper contact and minimal impedance. Sessions are conducted in a controlled environment to reduce external noise and artifacts.

Data Analysis

Our data analysis pipeline includes preprocessing, feature extraction, and classification stages. We employ both traditional signal processing techniques and advanced machine learning methods to identify patterns in EEG data that correspond to different brain states.

Algorithm Development

We develop and test algorithms for brain-state classification, signal decoding, and control signal generation. Our approach combines theoretical modeling with empirical validation to ensure robust performance across different users and conditions.

Validation and Testing

We rigorously validate our methods through cross-validation, independent test sets, and real-world application testing. This ensures that our technology performs reliably in diverse scenarios and with different user populations.
